Travel Itinerary

Destination

Batangas City, Taal Heritage Town, Nasugbu

Duration of Stay

3 Days, 2 Nights

TRANSPORTATION

Bus, Car Rental, Ferry

ACTIVITIES

Beach Hopping, Scuba Diving, Cultural Tours

Day 01

BEACH ADVENTURE

What to do: Visit Nasugbu beaches and enjoy water sports.

Budget: ₱2,500

Experience the tranquility of seaside serenity at Canyon Cove Hotel and Spa, a gem nestled in the picturesque municipality of Nasugbu, Batangas. Just a two-hour journey from Metro Manila, The resort serves as sanctuary from the hustle of city life, offering exquisite views of the West Philippine Sea and a breathtaking stretch of fine white sand.
The distinctive features include a safeguarding breakwater, Batangas’ largest recreational swimming pool, and a myriad of exhilarating water activities. Embrace an eco-friendly escapade filled with outstanding hospitality, where each stay promises unforgettable memories and experiences to cherish.

Day 02

CULTURAL EXPLORATION

What to do: Explore Taal Heritage Town.

Budget: ₱1,800

Taal is a municipality of old people in Batangas. The old downtown (Poblacion) is noted for its Spanish colonial architecture, and is designated as a National Historical Landmark by the Philippine government. Being about a two-hour drive from Metro Manila, Taal is an easy getaway from the concrete jungle and chaos there, a much closer alternative to the better-known heritage town Vigan. There are many tourist-oriented businesses; you may find a heritage house with a cafe or a bed and breakfast in it. Even the 7-Eleven store near the basilica is built in colonial-style architecture to blend with the surroundings.
Taal is just across a small river from the larger town of Lemery. Most of Taal, including the downtown, is inland on higher ground to the southeast, but Butong barangay is along the coast south of Lemery.

Day 03

DIVING AND FAREWELL

What to do: Scuba diving in Anilao.

Budget: ₱3,000

Anilao is a barangay (small administrative district) in the municipality of Mabini, Batangas, that is dubbed as the birthplace of scuba diving in the Philippines. It sits about 90 KM from Manila City, which makes it one of the most popular quick weekend getaways for tourists, especially diving enthusiasts, due to its vibrant underwater scenery.
Many experienced divers call it a paradise for underwater macro photography as well as a treasure trove for colorful and unique nudibranch species. There are more than 20 known dive sites in Anilao, including The Cathedral, Arthur’s Rock, Twin Rocks, Ligpo Island, Koala, Bethlehem, Beatrice Rock, Devil’s Point, Layag-Layag, and Kirby’s Rock, among others.
